Talk Title

Noise, metrics, and construct validity in multimodal model benchmarking

Abstract

There are many overlooked problems in multimodal model evaluation; in this talk I will focus on three interconnected problems: dataset noise, benchmark construct validity, and underspecified metrics. Sample noise is one driver of the much recognized “saturation point”: a sub-100% performance number that shapes the effective ceiling of a system’s performance on some benchmark.

However, noise does not just produce a ceiling—it introduces artifacts across the dynamic range. Noise has multiple drivers, including sloppy annotations and poorly designed metrics. In the second half of the talk, I will cover how to interrogate whether an evaluation metric actually captures the phenomenon it purports to measure.

These ideas motivate broader questions about what is missing in modern evaluation, and what philosophies should drive benchmark design.
