Talk Title

The Impact of 30+ Years of Face Recognition Competitions

Abstract

Competitions have become a core method in the fields of face recognition, biometrics, computer vision, and artificial intelligence. In 1993, the FERET program introduced competitions to computational face recognition, establishing a framework that has both measured and driven progress in the field.

This lecture will cover: (1) the role of competitions in face recognition; (2) their impact on algorithmic development—where identification error rates for mugshot-style images have halved approximately every two years over the past three decades; (3) how face recognition competitions contributed to the deep learning revolution in AI; (4) comparisons between human and algorithmic performance; and (5) approaches to fusing human and algorithmic decision-making.
