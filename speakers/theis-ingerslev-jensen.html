Talk Title

Replication Crises and the Common Task Framework

Abstract

Research progresses by new research building on old, as emphasized by Newton when he said, “if I have seen further than others, it is by standing on the shoulders of giants.” But knowing which shoulders to stand on is complicated by issues with comparability and research distortions, such as p-hacking, and recently, there have been several reported replication crises in fields like medicine, psychology, and finance.

I show that the crisis in finance is nuanced: the stock return predictability literature replicates well, whereas the sustainable finance literature replicates poorly. Further, I show that a methodology from computer science—the common task framework (CTF)—can help address issues of comparability and distortion, thereby reducing the risk of a replication crisis and accelerating scientific innovation.

A key element of the CTF is its focus on shared competitions, code, and data, and I showcase several examples in finance, including a new CTF-inspired competition and an open-source dataset of stock returns and firm characteristics spanning 100 years and 93 countries.
