Talk Title

Automated Decision Making for Safety-Critical Applications

Abstract

Building robust decision-making systems for autonomous platforms is challenging. Decisions must be made under uncertainty — with imperfect information about the environment and incomplete knowledge of how it will evolve. These systems must balance safety with operational efficiency, while navigating vast spaces of edge cases that designers must anticipate.

This talk discusses major challenges associated with ensuring computational tractability and establishing trust that autonomous decision systems will behave correctly when deployed in the real world. We will outline methodologies for addressing these challenges and highlight research applications that illustrate pathways toward safer systems.

Bio

Mykel J. Kochenderfer is an Associate Professor of Aeronautics and Astronautics at Stanford University and director of the Stanford Intelligent Systems Laboratory (SISL), where his group develops advanced algorithms and analytical frameworks for robust decision making. His research centers on efficient computational methods for deriving optimal decision strategies from high-dimensional probabilistic representations.

Prior to joining Stanford in 2013, Prof. Kochenderfer worked at MIT Lincoln Laboratory, where he led development of aircraft collision avoidance systems, resulting in the ACAS X international standard for manned and unmanned aviation.

He is co-director of the Center for AI Safety, editor-in-chief of the Journal of Artificial Intelligence Research, and author of several MIT Press textbooks including Decision Making under Uncertainty (2015), Algorithms for Optimization (2019), and Algorithms for Decision Making (2022).
