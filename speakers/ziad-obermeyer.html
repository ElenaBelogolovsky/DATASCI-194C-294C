Talk Title

Nature as the Referee: Benchmarking AI on Real Patient Data

Abstract

At your next doctor’s visit, the visit time and duration may have been determined by an algorithm, and you may see your doctor using an AI scribe to write her note. But despite widespread adoption of AI for administrative tasks, it’s unlikely your doctor will use any AI tools to make better decisions about your health. The striking absence of clinical AI is rooted in some specific challenges to building machine learning in medical data: patient records are locked up inside of healthcare institutions, and fundamental quantities of interest - for example “health” - are un- or mis-measured. We’ll talk through some of the problems this creates, and outline some emerging solutions.
